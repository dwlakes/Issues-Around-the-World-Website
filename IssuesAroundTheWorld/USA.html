While the US is a prosperous developed country, the US is home to a plethora of social issues including but not limited to wealth and income inequality, poverty, drug addiction, homelessness, inadequate healthcare, and racial disparities manifesting across many issues.

Poverty

The US Census reports that over 1 in 10 people in the US live in poverty, with the poverty rate being at 11.4%. 1 The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) reports that amongst the 39 developed nations that has information available, the U.S. has the 6th highest childhood poverty rate, with the childhood poverty rate being at 21.2% in 2017 compared to Denmark at 4.7% in the same year and Finland at 3.5% in 2018. 2 When it comes to overall poverty, the OECD has the U.S. ranked with at 2nd with a 17.8% poverty in 2017, compared to Iceland with the lowest poverty rate at 4.9%.2 Poverty in the US is also stratisfied across race with the poverty rates of minorities often being over twice as high as their White counterparts. For example, in 2019 the Kaiser Family Foundation puts Black poverty rate at 21.2%, Hispanic at 17.2%, and Native American at 24.2%, while the poverty rate amongst Whites is at 9%.3

Drug Addiction

The US has one of the highest overdose rates in the world.4 The Center for Disease Control (CDC) reports that in 2019 the U.S. had 21.6 overdose deaths per 100,000 people5 while the European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction (EMCDDA) reported 1.4 overdose deaths per 100,000 people for the same year.6
