Romania

  • Poverty
  • Drug Addiction

Poverty

The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development(OECD) reports that amongst the 39 developed nations that has information available, Romania has the2nd highest childhood poverty rate, with the childhood poverty rate being at 23.1% in 2018 compared to Finland at 4.7% in the same year and Denmark at 3.5% in 2018. 1 When it comes to overall poverty, the OECD has Romania ranked with at 3rd with a 17.4% poverty in 2017, compared to Iceland with the lowest poverty rate at 4.9%.1 It is estimated that 6,000 homeless children live in the sewers of Romania, with many being born in the sewers.2

Drug Addiction

The European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction (EMCDDA) reports that Romania tends to have lower overdose rates than the rest of Europe, with about 30 overdose deaths per year, or about .16 ovevdose deaths per 100,000 people,3 while the European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction (EMCDDA) reported 1.4 overdose deaths per 100,000 people for the same year.4 In 2019, the EMCDDA reported 49% of drug treatments entrances in Romania were for Cannabis, 24% for heroin, 24% for "other", 1% for amphetamines, and 1% for cocaine.3 Perhaps the most concerning issue when it comes to drug use in Romania is that the aforementioned children that live in sewers sniff glue and paint to alievate hunger.5
